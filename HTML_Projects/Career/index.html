Why Coding?

How did we get here?, Apr 1, 2019

The decision to switch gears and get into coding is not one I took lightly. In fact, I had been toying with it since 2014. Back then I had just left the company I was working for and wanted to explore coding, but wasn't in the best position to 1. spend money on a bootcamp and 2. not have an income. So I shelved the idea and took another job that turned out to be great, albeit in the same function that I was in previously. A few years passed, I was successful in that role, and then stepped into a leadership role. Again, further ingratiating myself into that role and function. It became too late to stop what I was doing.

I accepted a job at ActiveCampaign that seemed great, but the more I settled in, the more I realized my focus would be in areas I didn't have expertise in, but more importantly, any interest in. The reality is, I can do the job but I don't want to do the job. Thankfully, having recently been married and doing a better job of saving over the past years, I was in a position to re-evaluate my career and jump on my wife's insurance while I did so. Enter coding. I was able to start committing time to it on CodeAcademy and feel out the different languages available to me. I studied the basics; HTML, CSS, Javascript, but also Ruby and Python. Python was the one I found most interesting and preferred the syntax of. With a great support system, I was encouraged to give this a shot as I've always been interested in the T&P side of the orgs I've worked in. Now I'm going to see if I can hang!

Career History

My Journey, Apr 1, 2019

A long, winding road...

Right out of college, I accepted a sales job with CDW and began my career. I learned a ton about how to sell, all the technology that CDW could provide customers, and that I hated being in a cold-calling sales role. Well, that and the fact that I worked in the suburbs (Vernon Hills, IL) and lived with my parents. Not the coolest start to a career. My friend worked at Groupon and was heavily suggesting I take a job there, move to the city, and start living my life. I took his suggestion, jumped over to Groupon, and had my first taste of Chicago's tech scene. Me and literally everyone in Chicago around the age of 22 was working there. It was also sales, but had such a different vibe to it. I loved it, despite not liking what I did. After a few years of being a part of taking the company public, moving up to the premier team, New York, and honing my skills, I wanted a smaller experience.

I took a job at Fooda, a company of 13 when I joined, and started doing some great work. The best proof of that is the fact that Fooda is now a multi-million dollar company making a huge footprint in Chicago and other cities. I'm proud of the work I did there, opening new markets and making a small company something much bigger. Things ultimately didn't work out as we grew and I moved to Raise, a gift card reselling platform.

Raise was a wild exprience, one that I'll never forget. They also gave me an opportunity to lead my own team, which was huge in my career development. I started not only helping our customers, but also helping my team achieve their personal goals, which was rewarding. Exhausting, too. After 3 years, we underwent some changes and eliminated my entire department. Bittersweet, but an opportunity for me to take what I learned to another company.

This is where I joined ActiveCampaign and fell into a role where I was both leading a team, but also developing the customer onboarding strategy for the entire company. This was not my wheelhouse, I had never onboarded customers to a marketing plaform, much less one that I didn't fully understand yet. It was in doing this that I began to realize I wanted something new. I had been following this career path as it came to me, but never stopped to think if it was what I wanted. After thinking it through, I decided it wasn't the right fit for me, and that I wanted more exposure to the T&P side of the business. I wouldn't be able to get there without some new skills, so I started researching bootcamps and ended up here.
